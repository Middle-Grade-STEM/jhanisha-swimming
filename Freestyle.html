What is Freestyle?

This image credit goes to allamwericanswim.com

Freestyle is a stroke that is used to swim fast laps without losing all your energy.

What is the technique for freestyle?

The technique for freestyle can be a bit intimidating for beginners. Freestyle is also called the front crawl. When you swim freestyle you are on your stomach and your face toward the water while your arms and legs pull your legs through the water. First, your arm arcs out while your other arm stays in the water. Your fingers are held together and straight. Your legs will be moving in a tight scissor-like movement under the water, like a flutter kick. Even though your knees will be bent slightly, your legs must remain parallel to the surface of the water all the time. For freestyle you must keep your head down which might be a bit scary for beginners but adding a breath to freestyle is very easy. As one arm comes out of the water you turn your face to the side and take a breath.

What are the benefits of freestyle?

Freestyle has a lot of benefits. Here are a few of the benefits
  • You can burn a lot of calories by swimming for just 30 mins
  • Like I said in the beginning you can swim freestyle without wasting too much energy. So, you if you are a swimmer who swims long-distance or want to swim a lot of laps freestyle is the best choice!
  • Freestyle is a stroke that will give you a full body workout.
