What is Butterfly?

Butterfly is the most challenging. You must have lot of strength and coordination which takes time to develop.

What are the techniques for Butterfly?

The name butterfly comes on how your arm moves for this stroke. You do this stroke by moving both your arms at the same time. Your arms come out of the like wings, except your thumb is facing towards the water. Your body is in the same position as freestyle and breaststroke-horizontal. Your hands sweep down and out to form a Y shape in front of the body, then you turn and sweep your hands back in towards each other, while keeping your elbows high. Next, you turn your hands up and back and sweep them parallel to the side of your body. To kick for butterfly, you use the dolphin kick. You must keep your legs together and let your heels and soles of your feet break the surface form under. Your knees must be bent slightly on the up kick. To breathe in butterfly, you must breathe while your hands are in the recovery motion. Make sure you inhale quickly.

What are the benefits of Butterfly?

You burn about 450 calories (about 36 minutes of running) when you swim for 30 minutes. This stroke works upper body training especially core training. This stroke challenges your core muscles because your body must be stable when you move your arms and legs. You also work your arm, chest, and upper back muscles because you must raise both your arms up out of the water. Your body's position also helps improve your flexibility.
